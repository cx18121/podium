## Design Context

### Users
Professionals and presenters using Podium to practice and improve their delivery. They record a session, receive annotated video playback with ML-derived scores, and track improvement over time. They open the app focused and purposeful — they want signal, not distraction. They're self-motivated, data-literate, and pressed for time.

### Brand Personality
**Precise, Calm, Professional.**

Podium is the trusted coach in your corner. It doesn't celebrate you or scold you — it gives you the truth clearly and calmly. The interface is a neutral, focused workspace that gets out of the way and lets the data speak.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Color System — Slate + Indigo**

|------|-------|-----|
| Background | Deep blue-slate | `#080c14` |
| Surface | Elevated panels | `#0f1629` |
| Surface raised | Hover / active | `#14203a` |
| Accent | Indigo-500 | `#6366f1` |
| Accent hover | Indigo-400 | `#818cf8` |
| Text primary | Slate-100 | `#f1f5f9` |
| Text secondary | Slate-400 | `#94a3b8` |

**Theme:** Dark-only. No light mode planned.

**References:**
- **Loom** — Video-centric, approachable, human warmth without being casual. The product feels like a thoughtful collaborator.
- **Framer** — Motion-forward, premium, slick transitions. Animations feel crafted, not applied. Details that make you ask "how did they do that?"

**Anti-references:** Avoid the raw analytics dashboard look (Bloomberg, Grafana), avoid anything that feels like a developer CLI tool, avoid playful/gamified aesthetics.

### Design Principles

1. **Calm signal, no noise.** Every element earns its place. If it doesn't help the user understand their performance or take action, it should not be there. Reduce cognitive load at every turn.

2. **Three surfaces, consistent depth.** Background → surface → raised. Create spatial hierarchy through layering and subtle borders, not box-shadow stacking. Depth should feel structural, not decorative.

3. **Motion as communication.** Animations are purposeful — entrance animations orient, transitions maintain context, micro-interactions confirm actions. Inspired by Framer's precision. Never animate for spectacle alone.

4. **Human warmth in a data-heavy product.** Inspired by Loom's approachability — the interface acknowledges there's a real person on the other side, practicing something they care about. Score displays, feedback moments, and empty states should feel encouraging, not clinical.

5. **Indigo owns CTA, semantics own score.** Indigo-500 is reserved for primary calls to action and interactive highlights. Score colors (emerald / amber / red) are a separate semantic system and must not be used decoratively.

### Accessibility
Best-effort. Avoid obvious failures. No hard WCAG target. Sighted-user focus for now, with keyboard navigation where straightforward.
